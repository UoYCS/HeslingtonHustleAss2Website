Responsibility-Driven-Design (RDD) was the method used to design our object-oriented game system. This method allowed us to organise what potential objects were needed in our game and by designating roles and responsibilities to each object it allowed us to achieve our requirements in the best way. We followed the steps of RDD to design the Class Responsibility Collaborator (CRC) cards: we gave a purpose and role to each candidate object; we grouped and defended their inclusion; we identified the responsibilities of each candidate object and how they collaborated with each other and finally iterated the process till our model satisfied the requirements we had set out.

The first version of our CRC cards mapped out the relationships between the objects we would need and the object groups that were formed framed our initial class system.
